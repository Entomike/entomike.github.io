Texas Treehoppers - Family Membracidae

Deitz and Wallace (2012) report literature records for some 75 spp. of membracids in Texas,
though this is probably a gross under estimate due to the lack of Texas membracid publications.
Nearly 50 spp. are represented here.

I wish to thank Andy Hamilton for his gracious assistance with many of the following ID's,
but any errors here are mine alone.

Specimen photos from the TAMUIC and the UTIC collections

Subfamily Centrotinae

- Tylocentrus reticulatus Van Duzee - Range: sw US

Selenacentrus wallacei Morris & Dietrich - Range: TX, n. Mex.

Subfamily Darninae

= - Stictopelta marmorata Goding - Range: sw US to s. TX
== - Stictopelta varians Fowler - Range: CA-AZ, c. TX

Subfamily Stegaspidinae

= - Microcentrus perditus (Amyot & Serville) - Range: e NA to NM / ne Mex.

Subfamily Membracinae

  • Tribe Hoplophorionini

== - Oak Treehopper - Platycotis vittata (Fabricius) - Range: e. US, AZ & w. coast
== - Thorn Treehopper - Umbonia crassicornis (Amyot & Serville) - Range: FL peninsula, southmost TX

  • Tribe Membracini

- Campylenchia latipes (Say) - widely dist. across N. Amer.
- Two-marked Treehopper - Enchenopa binotata complex - Range: e. NA to AZ
= - Tylopelta gibbera (Stål) - se. US to AZ

Subfamily Smilinae

  • Tribe Acutalini

== - Acutalis tartarea (Say) - Range: e. NA to UT

  • Tribe Amastrini

    • Vanduzea spp.
=== - Vanduzea segmentata (Fowler) - Range: s US to S. Amer.
= - Vanduzea triguttata (Burmeister) - Range: e. US to CA

  • Tribe Ceresini

    • Ceresa spp.  (genus sometimes split into other genera, 9 spp. of Ceresa (sensu lato) reported from OK)
= - Buffalo Treehopper - Ceresa alta Walker - one of the few pest species - Range: e NA + scattered w. NA records
== - Ceresa taurina (Fitch) - Range: e. NA to AZ
= - Three-cornered Alfalfa Hopper - Ceresa festina Say - hosts on var. legumes & grasses - Range: widely dist. (even in the south)
== - Ceresa inermis (Fabricius) - Range: widely dist. across NA (Note: black spots are anomalies)

  • Tribe Micrutalini

    • Micrutalis spp.
- Micrutalis parva (Goding) - Range: s. US
- Micrutalis calva (Say) - Range: e NA to NM

  • Tribe Polyglyptini

- Publilia modesta (Uhler) - Range: w. NA
- Polyglypta dorsalis Burmeister - Range: TX, GA (This is the only online photo of this species.)

  • Tribe Smiliini - (includes many specialists on oaks (Quercus spp.)

    • Archasia spp.
- Archasia auriculata (Fitch) - Range: e NA to NM
= - Archasia pallida (Fairmaire) - Range: e US to AZ
    • Cyrtolobus spp. (18 spp. reported from OK)
= - Cyrtolobus arcuatus (Emmons) - Range: e. US to CO
- Cyrtolobus dixianus Woodruff - Range: e NA
- Cyrtolobus fenestratus (Fitch) - Range: e. NA
- Cyrtolobus fuliginosus (Emmons) - Range: e. NA
- Cyrtolobus maculifrontis (Emmons) - Range: e. NA
- Cyrtolobus ovatus Van Duzee - Range: e. US
- Cyrtolobus parvulus Woodruff - Range: e. US
- Cyrtolobus tuberosus (Fairmaire) - Range: e. NA
    • Smilia spp.
== - Smilia camelus (Fabricius) - Range: e NA, CA
= - Smilia fasciata Amyot & Serville - Range: e US
    • Entylia
= - Entylia carinata (Forster) - Range: e. NA to S. Amer.
    • Ophiderma spp. (7 spp. reported from OK)
- Ophiderma evelyna Woodruff - Range: e. NA
- Ophiderma grisea Woodruff - Range: e. US
- Ophiderma flavicephala Goding - Range: e NA
    • Xantholobus spp. (5 spp. reported from OK)
- Xantholobus muticus (Fabricius) - Range: e. NA (aberrant individual)

  • Tribe Telamonini - (includes many specialists on oaks (Quercus spp.)

    • Carynota
=== - Carynota mera (Say) - Range: e. NA
    • Glossonotus spp.
= - Glossonotus acuminatus (Fabricius) - Range: e. NA
= - Glossonotus turriculatus (Emmons) - e. NA to NM
    • Heliria
= - Heliria cristata (Fairmaire) - Range: e. US
    • Palonica

    • Telamona spp. (genus sometimes split into other genera)
== - Telamona concava Fitch - Range: e NA
=== - Telamona excelsa (Fairmaire) - Range: e. US
- Telamona maculata Van Duzee - Range: e. NA but uncommon in the south
= - Telamona monticola (Fabricius) - Range: e. NA to NM-NV
= - Telamona projecta Butler - Range: e US
== - Telamona reclivata Fitch - Range: e. NA. females have longer crests.
- Telamona tristis Fitch- Range: e. NA, but uncommon in the south.
