The study of Mexican butterflies has a long and rich history, with participants

from many nations. What follows is a partial list of literature related to

Mexican butterflies, primarily by Mexican authors; other works are listed if

contents are exclusively related (or nearly so) to Mexican butterflies. Being

largely in Spanish, the sources listed herein are often overlooked by those

seeking information on the history, taxonomy and systematics of Mexican

butterflies, yet present a tremendous wealth of information for those who seek

them out (and get a good English/Spanish dictionary if needed!). This list is

by no means intended to be a complete list, but is intended to highlight a few

works that contain a great deal of valuable information that are focused on

Mexican butterflies specifically. Hundreds of additional sources could have

been listed, that treat Mexican butterflies as well as those of other regions,

including generic revisions, older descriptions, phylogenetic analyses using

Mexican material, etc. Literature sources that are exclusively related to the

Monarch (Danaus plexippus) and Mexican Megathymini (Giant Skippers), which

include several hundred additional citations, each, are not included (with two

exceptions); nor are past Lepidopterists’ Society Season Summary reports for

the Neotropics or Xerces/NABA butterfly count reports for the Neotropics. A

complete list of all literature dealing with any aspect of Mexican butterflies

is being prepared by Llorente et al. in Mexico City. I apologize in advance

for any typos or erroneous page or volume numbers herein.
