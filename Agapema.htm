Chihuahuan Agapema

Agapema anona dyari Cockerell, 1914

Family Saturniidae, Subfamily Saturniinae, Tribe Saturniini

 

Brantley Lake SP Access Road, Eddy Co., New Mexico
~ 10 miles N of Carlsbad, 
23 May 2005 (Michael Van Buskirk)

Texas County Records for Agapema dyari

Mapping Software created by Joe Lapp, a.k.a. Spider Joe

Range: W. Texas, SE New Mexico; Chihuahua, Mexico

Flight Period: Emergence peaks from October-November.

Larval Plant Host: Javelina Bush - Condalia ericoides - Family Rhamnaceae
                                    Synonym: Microrhamnus ericoides

U.S. Agapema Taxa:

Agapema anona complex: (County distributions per Texas Lepidoptera Survey, Feb. 2007)

Agapema anona anona - southern AZ
Agapema anona dyari - Texas: El Paso, Hudspeth, Reeves, Culberson, Presidio, Jeff Davis, Pecos, and Terrell.
Agapema anona platensis - Val Verde, Uvalde, Maverick, Zavala, Frio, Zavalla, Dimmit, McMullen, Webb, and Kinney.
Intergrades of platensis/dyari occur in Kerr, Kimble, Menard, Mason, and Howard. 

Agapema galbina - Cameron, Hidalgo Counties, TX (historical records); northern Mexico
Agapema homogena - Rocky Mountains: AZ, CO, NM, Jeff Davis Co., TX, western Mexico
Agapema solita - 

Two other Agapema taxa occur from Mexico to Central America.
