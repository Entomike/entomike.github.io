The carabid was probably collected by Wheeler, Melander or Brues who were all at UT Austin for a few yrs in the early 1900's. Who ID'ed it (and when) as A. affabilis is more of an open question. Chris Durden, curator emeritus at the Texas Memorial Museum on the UT campus, says all spmns from that era were either dispersed to other institutions or destroyed by insect pests. Judging from Bousquet's description of the condition of the type, it seems that it barely escaped the later fate...
