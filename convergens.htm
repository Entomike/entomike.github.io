Convergent Lady Beetle

Hippodamia convergens Guérin-Méneville, 1842

 

Larva eating an Aphid (Aphis nerii)

 

 

 

 

 

Edinburg, Hidalgo Co., TX 
March 19, 2005 (Cat Traylor)

Range: Gymnetis caseyi occurs in Texas, Louisiana, Tamaulipas & San Luis Potosí.

Flight Period: There are 77 G. caseyi specimens in Texas A&M collection collected between March and December, most from May to September.

Month March April May June July Aug. Sept. Oct. Nov. Dec.
Texas 2 0 14 19 19 6 8 3 0 1
Mexico* 4 1

*The limited number of Mexico records reflects, in part, limited collecting there.

Taxonomy: Per Arnett, et al. 2002, there is but one Gymnetis species in the US. Ratcliffe determined 18 spp. within Gymnetis.

Gymnetina cretacea (frequently incorrectly listed on various websites as Gymnetis cretacea)
occurs in Arizona and New Mexico. This species is similarly patterned, but black and white vs. yellow.
There may be a second, similar species of Gymnetina occurring in AZ-NM.
