Fireflies of Texas - Family Lampyridae

There are 40+ species of Lampyrids recorded from Texas (E.G. Riley, pers. comm., 2016)

Specimens primarily shot at the TAMUIC and the UTIC

Family Lampyridae (150-200 NA spp., 40+ TX spp.) (NA diversity numbers per Lloyd, 2003, TX diversity numbers per E.G. Riley, pers. comm., 2016)

Subfamily Lampyrinae (higher taxonomy follows Bouchard et al. 2011)

Tribe Lampyrini

  • Microphotus (7 NA spp., 2 TX spp.) (only the females emit light)

- Microphotus octarthrus Fall - AZ to w. TX (distributions per literature, specimen label data in the TAMU and UT collections, plus BugGuide data)
- Microphotus pecosensis Fall - sw US to w. TX / nc. Mex.

Tribe Pleotomini

  • Pleotomus (2 (3?) NA spp., 2 TX spp.) (only the females emit light)

==== - Pleotomus pallens LeConte - sc US (NM to AL, n. to KS), but most common in south and west TX
== - Pleotomus nigripennis LeConte - AZ to w. TX (?NSR)

Tribe Lamprocerini

  • Tenaspis (1 NA sp.)

- Tenaspis angularis (Gorham) - Gulf Coast to C. Amer. (diurnal, very rare)

Tribe Cratomorphini

  • Aspisoma (2 NA spp., 1 TX sp.)

= - Aspisoma ignitum (Linnaeus) - w. & s. TX to S. Amer. (very rare, known in Texas from three specimens)

  • Pyractomena (21 NA spp., 5+ TX spp.)

= - Pyractomena punctiventris (LeConte) - e., c. & s. TX
  - Pyractomena angulata (Say) - e NA to e. TX
   - Pyractomena vexillaria Gorham -

= - Ellychnia flavicollis (LeConte) - NM to c. TX

  • Lucidota (4 NA spp., 1 TX sp.)

==== - Lucidota atra (Olivier) - e. NA to e TX (diurnal)

  • Phausis (10+ NA spp., 1+ TX spp.)
