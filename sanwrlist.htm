Return to Texas Entomology - Compiled by Mike Quinn


Original data for this checklist compiled by Edward Knudson
Last updated by Mike Quinn on 06 March, 2011.

Although centered on Santa Ana, all records Valley-wide are listed here.


Not enough is yet known about the Lepidoptera of Santa Ana National Wildlife Refuge to permit the inclusion of seasonal distribution, but in general butterflies tend to be more abundant in the late summer and fall. The relative abundance or rarity of species is indicated by the table given below.

A - Abundant (Occurring year round in large to very large numbers)
C - Common (Occurring most of the year in moderate to occasionally large numbers)
U - Uncommon (Seasonal, occurring in small to moderate numbers)
O - Occasional (Probably not present every year, but occasionally present in moderate numbers)
R - Rare (Not usually present, when found only in small numbers)
X - Accidental (Recorded only once or twice, probably as a stray)
H - Hypothetical (Not recorded from Santa Ana, but has been found in similar habitats nearby, or recorded as accidental from the tropics to the north of the area)
