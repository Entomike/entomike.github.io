Palmetto Longhorn

Osmopleura chamaeropis (Horn 1893)

Family Cerambycidae, Subfamily Cerambycinae, Tribe Agallissini

First State Record

Dorsal - High resolution photo 

Ventral - Discussion of Behavior

Port O'Connor, Calhoun County, Texas
June 07, 2007 (Brush Freeman)

One individual was found on the fishing pier in Port O'Connor, 
Possibly attracted to the pier's fishing lights the previous night.  

Texas Palmetto Beetle County Record

 

Beetle only known in Texas from Port O'Connor, Calhoun County

POC has no international ports and about the only Gulf traffic is of a few crew and shrimp boats.
However, the Intercoastal Waterway is about 0.5 miles from where the beetle was collected.

Palmetto grove near point of beetle discovery 

Dwarf Palmetto - Sabal minor grove near where the Palmetto Beetle was discovered.
S. minor is a possible second host for the palm beetle's larvae.

S. minor is scattered throughout Port O'Connor, as seen above. 

There's a veritable forest of S. minor just east of Tivoli, 
in Calhoun and extreme SE Refugio Counties, TX, on the Guadalupe River Delta.
This palm forest is about 22 miles from Port O'Connor as the crow (beetle?) flies.
Similar habitat exists in riparian areas in Calhoun, Jackson and Refugio Counties.
Sabal minor - Texas County Records - USDA

Range: Florida, Georgia, and now this record from Texas. Florida distribution of Osmopleura chamaeropis.

Adult Activity: May-June.

Larval Host:

In Florida Osmopleura chamaeropis feeds on Cabbage Palmetto - Sabal palmetto - The State Tree of Florida
S. palmetto is mostly restricted to the Florida peninsula and coastal Georgia (see range map below). 
(In Florida, the distribution of Osmopleura chamaeropis overlaps the distribution of Sabal palmetto.)
If O. chamaeropis is found breeding in Dwarf Palmetto - Sabal minor, this would be a new host record.

Sabal minor - Texas County Records - USDA

Habitat: In Florida, this beetle occurs in pine-palmetto woodlands.

Abundance: Rarely collected but can be locally abundant (Deyrup &. Franz, 1994; Turnbow & Hovore, 1979)

Size: 1.1 inches (28 mm) in length

Collecting Methods: 

Best to carefully search the host plants or use a malaise trap, Lindgren funnel, or flight intercept trap as this species is apparently not attracted to lights or sugar (Dan Heffern, pers. comm. 2007) Insect Collecting Information

Similar species: Osmopleura is a monotypic genus

Behavior:

Unusual defense behavior... It popped itself over and held it's body off the ground with its legs spread stiffly... It held this position for over 40 minutes and returned to it immediately if even slightly disturbed. (B. Freeman, pers. comm. 2007)

Doug Yanega (pers. comm. 2007): I especially like the defensive posture! One can only suppose that it is some sort of mimetic/aposematic display. If I had to put money on it, I'd say it's pretending to be a Bembix or Bicyrtes - viewed from a distance, that's what the belly markings most closely resemble, and those wasps rest poised on the ground in a posture similar to the one the beetle is in. Certainly a novel mimetic behavior.

Example: Bembix americana - Bembicine Tribe, Sand Wasps

Agallissus lepturoides (Chevrolat in Orbigny 1844) 

Agallissus lepturoides is associated with palms in Central America and Texas. The two Texas (Cameron Co.) records are from about 1900 and 1990. (Dan Heffern, pers. comm., 2007)

Today, the only remaining large stands of palms in Cameron Co. are Sabal mexicana within the Sabal Palm Audubon Center, and to a lesser extent at the Nature Conservancy's Southmost Preserve
